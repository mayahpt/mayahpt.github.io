Diagnosing Orthopedic Abnormalities in Patients: A machine-learning approach

The goal of this project is to create a software based technique to diagnose patients without the need of specialized professionals in a user-friendly approach. This would not serve as a final diagnosis but would allow the clinicians to better assess their patients by categorizing them in the most probable disease. The chosen dataset (https://www.kaggle.com/uciml/biomechanical-features-of-orthopedic-patients?select=column_3C_weka.csv) uses 6 different attributes to classify the patient:Â  (1) pelvic incidence,(2) pelvic tilt,(3) lumbar lordosis angle,(4) sacral slope,(5) pelvic radius and (6) grade of spondylolisthesis. Using these attributes the patients would be classified in three categories: normal, disk hernia, and spondylolisthesis. Disk Hernia occurs when intervertebral disks contained in the spine are pushed out, causing compression over the spinal nerves. Spondylolisthesis is a shifting of the spine vertebrae due to the instability caused by a crack or stress fracture. Both conditions are usually diagnosed by expensive and/or complicated methods such CT/CAT Scan, Electromyogram and MRI and have, generally, the same symptoms. To achieve the goal of the project, we will write Python scripts to classify the data using different algorithms: K-Nearest Neighbour, Naive Bayes, and Gaussian Process. We will, then, test the accuracy of methods, and identify which provides the best results.
